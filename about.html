Bonodopt

Bonodopt is a Nonprofit organization with the sole aim of helping bonobos and fighting their extinction. While we don't actually work with bonobos ourselves, our job is to support those on the frontline by freeing them completely from the need to raise funding.

Bonodopt was established by Vanessa Woods, an Australian science writer, author and journalist. She is best known for her work in comparing the different cooperative behaviors of bonobos and common chimpanzees.

A big part of her research was done in the world's only Bonobo Sanctuary Lola ya Bonobo, wich was founded by the Belgian conservationist Claudine André. She was working closely with Claudine André and team, and their project became increasingly important to her. Simultaneously, she was noticing how much time was taken away from their valuable work to raise funding. While they wanted to dedicate more time towards their beloved bonobos, this was necessary to be able to continue their work.

When it was time for Vanessa to leave the sanctuary, she decided to found Bonodopt in order to be able to help their project from the distance. Furthermore, she was able to free Claudine André and team from the need to raise funds completely. Instead they can concentrate all their efforts towards the fight to protect bonobos from their extinction.

Our two main responsibilities are to increase awareness around the endangerement of Bonobos as well as raising funding for our program in cooperation with Lola ya Bonobo.

Note from Vanessa

"I may be the founder of Bondopt, but I am not the real hero. For over 25 years, Claudine André has fought for the conservation and welfare of bonobos in Congo. Not only did she establish Lola ya Bonobo, the world’s only bonobo sanctuary with over 90 orphans from the bushmeat trade, but more than anyone else in the world, Claudine has worked tirelessly to convince everyone from Congolese hunters to politicians that bonobos are a national treasure worth protecting.

Like bonobos, Claudine is virtually unknown in America and the English speaking world. I hope Bonodopt, besides introducing our wonderful long lost cousins and raising means for their protection, will also pay tribute to a woman with more courage than I ever thought possible, and my first encounter with a truly selfless heart."

- Vanessa Woods, December 15, 2019
